Expert Resume Writing Tips

Learn how to create a compelling resume that gets you noticed

Quick Tips for Success

1. Tailor Your Resume

Customize your resume for each job application by matching keywords from the job description.

2. Use Action Verbs

Start bullet points with strong action verbs like "Achieved," "Implemented," "Led," or "Developed."

3. Quantify Achievements

Include specific numbers and percentages to demonstrate your impact (e.g., "Increased sales by 45%").

Format & Structure

Do

  • Keep it to 1-2 pages
  • Use consistent formatting
  • Include white space
  • Use clear section headings
  • Choose readable fonts (10-12pt)

Don't

  • Use fancy fonts or colors
  • Include personal photos (unless requested)
  • Make it too dense
  • Use complex layouts
  • Include irrelevant information

Content Writing Tips

Professional Summary

Write a compelling 3-4 line summary highlighting your key qualifications and career goals.

Work Experience

Focus on achievements rather than duties. Use the PAR format: Problem, Action, Result.

Skills Section

Include both hard skills (technical) and soft skills (interpersonal) relevant to the position.

Education

List your most recent education first. Include relevant coursework, honors, and GPA if above 3.5.

ATS (Applicant Tracking System) Tips

Use Standard Section Headings

Use clear, standard section headings like "Work Experience" instead of creative alternatives.

Include Keywords

Incorporate relevant keywords from the job description naturally throughout your resume.

Avoid Graphics

Use simple formatting and avoid images, tables, or graphics that ATS might not process correctly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Spelling and Grammar

Always proofread carefully and use spell check. Consider having someone else review your resume.

Outdated Information

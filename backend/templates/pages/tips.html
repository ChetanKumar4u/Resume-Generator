Organize Your Content

Start with your contact information, followed by a professional summary. Then list your experience, education, and skills in order of relevance to the position.

Keep it Focused

Limit your resume to 1-2 pages. Focus on relevant experience and achievements that match the job requirements.

Highlight Achievements

Use action verbs and quantify your achievements. Instead of listing duties, showcase results and specific contributions.

Use Keywords

Include industry-specific keywords from the job description to pass Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) and catch the recruiter's attention.

Consistent Formatting

Maintain consistent font sizes, spacing, and bullet points throughout your resume. Use clear headings to separate sections.

Make it Scannable

Use white space effectively and break up text into bullet points. Recruiters often scan resumes in seconds before deciding to read further.

Resume Writing Guide

1. Start with a Strong Summary

Your professional summary should be a compelling 2-3 sentence paragraph that highlights your key achievements, skills, and career goals. Think of it as your elevator pitch.

2. Craft Your Experience Section

List your work experience in reverse chronological order. Use action verbs and focus on achievements rather than duties. Quantify results whenever possible.

3. Highlight Your Skills

Include a mix of hard skills (technical abilities) and soft skills (interpersonal qualities). Ensure they're relevant to the position you're applying for.

4. Education and Certifications

List your highest degree first. Include relevant certifications, training, and professional development courses.

Resume Checklist

Contact Information

Full name, phone number, professional email, location, LinkedIn profile

Professional Summary

2-3 sentences highlighting key qualifications and career goals

Work Experience

Company names, dates, job titles, key achievements with metrics

Education

Degrees, institutions, graduation dates, relevant coursework

Skills

Technical skills, soft skills, language proficiencies
